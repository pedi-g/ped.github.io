Pedram Ghahramani

PhD student in evolutionary biology & ecology. I study biodiversity patterns, functional traits, and how ecosystems—especially mangroves—respond to environmental change.

About me

I investigate how biodiversity is structured across space and time, with a focus on functional diversity and the mechanisms that sustain ecosystem resilience. My work combines field sampling, quantitative ecology, and reproducible analysis in R and Python. I have led and contributed to research on macrobenthic communities in Persian Gulf mangrove systems and comparative studies of natural versus restored habitats.

Functional diversity Trait-based ecology Mangrove ecosystems Biodiversity time series Spatial analysis (QGIS)

Research

Ecosystem function in mangroves

Using trait-based metrics (e.g., FRic, FEve) and community composition to compare natural and planted mangroves. Analyses integrate environmental gradients (temperature, salinity, pH, sediment granulometry, TOM) across seasons and habitats (mangrove vs. mudflat).

  • Links between habitat heterogeneity and functional redundancy
  • Implications for restoration design and monitoring
  • Open, reproducible workflows

Current directions

  • Lepidoptera diversity change in time-series datasets
  • Light pollution as a driver of community reassembly
  • Trait syndromes linked to resilience under warming
